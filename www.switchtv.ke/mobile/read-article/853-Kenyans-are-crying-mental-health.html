Kenyans are crying mental health

Posted on Tuesday, June 8, 2021


It is high time Mental Health matters were addressed in Kenya. Kenya is an African country and most Africans believe that they cannot suffer from such issues. This has however been proven wrong by a series of incidents affecting some Kenyan celebrities, pointing to words mental health troubles and depression.  A few days ago, celebrated Kenyan musician and producer Maigix Enga raised concern amongst netizens after he posted a message on Instagram that came out as suicidal. The gengetone producer suggested that he was going to die that night. This made people think that he was planning on ending his life. 

‘I am going to die tonight. I am going to miss everyone.’ 

However, the following morning he came out to reassure his fans that he was just drunk. In a post made on Instagram he explained that he was just drunk and that led to the posts. 

“Heey, I’m not dead. I appreciate having such caring fans and family. I just can’t give up now. I was drunk zikanisho ni weke post ya umbwakni pombe sio supu mimi nimewacha,” 

This came as a relief to everyone who thought that he might have done something to himself considering he had earlier on enrolled himself in a rehabilitation center because of drug addiction that spiraled out of control. Since then, he has pulled down both posts.



In the same bandwagon we have popular you-tuber and content creator Elodie zone. In a post shared on blogger Edger Obare’s Instagram page, a screenshot of one of Elodie’s videos where one can clearly see cuts and bruises on her arm which had earlier been bandaged, was shared by a fan. Some netizens have since then come out to claim that she might be practicing self-harm A fan also posted a picture that Nvirii Elodie’s ex-boyfriend had posted on his Instagram story that highlighted borderline personality disorder which suggested that his partner might have mental health issues. 

Elodie And Nvirii have since been spotted together a few days after Elodie had announced that the two had broken up and even suggested that she had been in a toxic and abusive relationship. In a questions and answers session on her Instagram she said that she had hidden bruises from previous relationships and encouraged those in similar situations to speak up. 

“I’ve hidden bruises from my past two relationships. I’ve felt stupid and been undermined. I’ve kept quiet. I’ve been told to stop speaking up on social media coz ill look like I'm acting out. I’ve been told that depression means you overreact...etc... all are total nonsense. I want men and women in my position to raise their voices! We may be small but our voices cannot be silenced.” 

 Mental health is a very serious issue and especially since the rice of suicide cases as a result of depression amongst the youth have been on the rise in the country. We can only encourage everyone to check on their friend, families and their mental health.
