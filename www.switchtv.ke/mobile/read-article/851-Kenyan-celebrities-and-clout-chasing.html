Kenyan celebrities and clout chasing

Posted on Friday, June 4, 2021


Clout chasing has become a norm for our Kenyan celebrities. Top on our list is Ringtone Apoko. The self proclaimed chairman of the gospel industry claimed that he ushered Willy Paul into Christianity. Speaking at a popular TV show, he claimed that he is the one who led musicians willy Paul and Bahati to find  Christ.
 
'Willy Paul tumekupea miezi tatu ufanye hio maneno yako yesu anakuita urudi kwa yesu na uambie rafiki yako bahati mrudi kwa Mungu.'
 
However, Willy Paul refuted the claims in the same show adding that that his fellow musician Bahati  ‘baha’ might have  been the one directed by Ringtone.
 
“Baha ndio ameshikwa mkono..si mimi …mimi ni mungu wangu amenishikilia mkono”


 
Ringtone is not new to such controversies, just a week ago during Esther Musila’s birthday party and engagement to popular gospel artist Guardian Angel, the gospel chairman claimed that he would expose Guardian Angel for his hypocrisy. Instead the duo dropped a song dubbed ‘Fagia’.This publicity stunt might have been aimed at raising the songs viewership and it worked since the song has accumulated thousands of views in just six days. 
 
Another celebrity who has recently pulled such a stunt is ‘Nairobi youngest sponsor’ Shaqtheyoungin. The host posted a screenshot of his DM on Instagram in which Kylie Jenner was complimenting him. The radio presenter went further and posted a picture of him and kylie, adding a rather sarcastic love caption, tagging her.
 
While it is not clear what Shaq wants to accomplish with this it is clear that Kenyan celebrities are doing the most when it comes to improving their publicity. Others have gone as far as engaging in obscenities for fame. Recently, a wannabe celebrity Embarambara received harsh comments after a raunchy video of him dancing with revelers did its way around social media. Earlier on the artist who is known for his funny videos in which he dances in mud received an endorsement with a detergent company.
 
So ,how much is too much?
