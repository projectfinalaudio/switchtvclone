Coincidence or copy cat

Posted on Thursday, June 17, 2021


Over the past few weeks several artists' songs have been pulled down from youtube due to copyright claims. For this to happen it means that copyright infringement has occurred. Copyright infringement is a situation where a person uses someone else's work without permission. A lot of artists have come out to claim that their works have been copied. This has led to some of their songs being pulled down from various platforms. Although some artists don’t go the legal way, they make sure to announce it on various media platforms. Latest on our list is singer Octoppizzo popularly known as ‘Mr Ohanga’. Octoppizo through a tweet seemed to insinuate that Khaligraph Jones had copied his work.

 

“Ya Boyz couldn’t walk in my shoes if we shared legs.Keep Copy Pasting “

 

A keen fan noted that Khalighraph’s new song “nikiwa ni shoke” which was released two days ago is similar to Mr. Ohanga’s ‘Jump pydu’ which was released six years ago. Some netizens agree and others do not  but everyone is encouraged to watch and compare the two videos for personal gratification. The two artist have always had a musical beef.



Another artist who has been affected by this is genge artist Mejja otherwise known as ‘Mtoto wa khadija’. The “jana kuliendaje” hitmaker took to social media to express his frustrations after his latest song ‘tabia za wakenya’ was pulled down from youtube due to copyright claims. The song was gaining traction and had already surpassed the one million mark. He  posted a snippet of the other song on his instagram and could not understand how youtube could make this move and yet according to him the song he was accused of copying was nothing close to his. He promised his fans that he would not faze and would handle the matter head on.

 

“I am speechless nimetumiwa kwa mail na youtube… i will never give in im done being the humble guy hii nadeal nayo”
